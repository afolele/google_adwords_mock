Bears

The Energy Savers

Most species of bears hibernate during the cold winter months. This is nature’s version of “sleep” mode, when bears power off (actually lowering their heart rate to around 8 beats a minute) and use energy so well they avoid having to eat or drink.

How Google is inspired by nature's engineers

  • Commuting

    Our biodiesel shuttles transport over 6,400 Googlers to work every day, and we have the largest corporate electric vehicle charging system in the country.

  • Saving Energy

    Our data centers use 50% less power than the average data center. One of the many ways we do this is through free cooling, which uses outside air or recycled water to reduce heat.

  • Eating Local

    Whenever possible we source organic and sustainable food locally at all our campuses around the world.

  • Composting

    We use compost-friendly packaging on all our campuses. 80% of the waste from our cafes is composted and the rest is recyclable.

  • Renewable Power

    We buy electricity from wind farms near our data centers and we’ve made agreements to fund $1.5 billion in renewable energy projects.
